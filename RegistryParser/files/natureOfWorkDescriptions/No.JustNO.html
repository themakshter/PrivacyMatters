Nature of work -

 

Community Energy Direct (CED) Nature of Work description

 

CED is an industrial and provident society owned by its members, who are community interest bodies, with consumer-facing operations.

 

CED and its members are all bound by their constitutions to focus on achieving the following principles

 

• delivering cost-effective energy;

 

• promoting the development and community ownership of low carbon energy                     generation assets;

 

• increasing community awareness of resource efficiency and sustainable development.

 

CED’s members are referred to below as Community Energy Societies.

CED’s activities include:

 

• to promote and encourage within the United Kingdom the formation and development of Community Energy Societies;

 

• to promulgate principles, on which Community Energy Societies should operate and to issue guidance on best practice for Community Energy Societies in relation to financial management, accounting standards, corporate governance, openness, efficiency, democracy or any other aspect of the affairs of Community Energy Societies;

 

• to comply with and monitor compliance by members with the Association’s principles;

 

• to act as an association or representative body of Community Energy Societies in dealing with central, regional and local government, statutory bodies, regulators and such other bodies as the Association decides from time to time;

 

• to act as a representative body for Community Energy Societies in order to negotiate commercial arrangements with third parties in the energy market

 

• to protect, promote and further the interests of Community Energy Societies;

  

• to provide advice and services for payment or free of charge to Community

Energy Societies in relation to any aspect of their affairs;

 

• to assist in the resolution of disputes between Members;

 

• to consider draft rules submitted by an industrial and provident society applying for membership of the Association, and rule changes proposed by members in the light of the Association’s Principles and to exercise any power granted under the Rules of any Community Energy Society.

 

Practical examples of these activities, and how they may involve personal data are set out below:-

 

• Introducing consumers either directly or indirectly (via its members) to switching services providers such as Which!

 

• Running consumer energy switching schemes

 

• Providing administrative support services for local smart-energy clubs, created by local community bodies such as housing associations and local authorities, whose aim is to attract consumers and encourage them to adopt energy efficient practices and participate in group energy-switching schemes

 

• Surveying consumer attitudes to energy and energy usage

 

• Creating and running websites as necessary to support the above actions

 

• CED is in partnership with the Department of Energy and Climate change (DECC), concerning elements of its work. All information shared with DECC as a result of CED’s work will be provided on an anonymised, aggregated basis.
