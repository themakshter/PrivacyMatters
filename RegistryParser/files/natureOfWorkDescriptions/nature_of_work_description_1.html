Nature of work - Expert Witness - Financial Services

 

Reasons/purposes for processing information

We process personal information to enable us to undertake analysis of financial situations of individuals, which includes compiling reports and statements on the results of the analysis for courts of law and arbitration, as well as private settlement of disputes.

 

Type/classes of information processed

We process information relevant to the above reasons/purposes. This information may include:

  • personal details
  • family details
  • financial details
  • education and employment details

We may also receive sensitive classes of information that are unlikely to be required for our work, but are practically inseparable from the information that we do require.

 

 

Who the information is processed about

We process personal information about:

  • clients
  • clients' spouses and other family members
  • any person about whom our clients give us information

Who the information may be shared with

We sometimes need to share the personal information we process with the individual themself and also with other organisations. Where this is necessary we are required to comply with all aspects of the Data Protection Act (DPA). What follows is a description of the types of organisations we may need to share some of the personal information we process with for one or more reasons.

 

Where necessary or required we share information with:

  • clients
  • clients' legal or other representatives
  • courts of law and arbitration
  • other parties' experts
  • any organisation that legally compels us to provide information which is not protected by legal privilege

 

Transferring information overseas

We may transfer personal information outside the European Economic Area (EEA) in the event it becomes necessary for the discharge of our responsibilities to clients.
